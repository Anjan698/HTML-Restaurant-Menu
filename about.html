About Thailand Thailand is a country located in Southeast Asia, known to tourists because of its beautiful beaches and rich culture. We are neighbors to Cambodia, Myanmar, and Laos, with China and Vietnam close by as well. Thailand has a population of nearly 65 million residents, with over 9 million living in the capital city of Bangkok. English is widely spoken. Visitors to Thailand experience a culture that is robust and unique. Thailand is often visited by many Americans each year. Thailand offers bustling city street markets where Thai silk, jewelry, antiques, bamboo artifacts, tailor-made clothes and the traditionally hot and spicy food choices. National food specialties including a coconut milk soup called “Tom Kha” and the popular rice noodles dish “Pud Thai” made with shrimp or chicken. On December 26, 2004, Thailand was hit by its worst natural disaster. An earthquake in the Indian Ocean that set off the three tsunami’s that struck the country, resulting in thousands of deaths and over 350 million U.S. Dollars in damage. Thailand has rebuilt itself to once again, become a favorite vacation spot for tourists
